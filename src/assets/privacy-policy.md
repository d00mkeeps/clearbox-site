# Clear Box Privacy Policy

**Last updated: 23 June 2026**

This privacy policy explains how Clear Box ("the app", "we") handles your data. It is written to be read, not skimmed past, because medication data is sensitive and you deserve to know exactly what happens to it.

## Who we are

Clear Box is operated by Miles Hillary, an individual data controller based in the United Kingdom. You can contact us at **hello@clearbox.info** for any privacy-related questions, data queries, or complaints. We aim to respond within 7 days and will respond within 30 days as required by UK GDPR.

## What data we collect

**A device identifier.** When you first open Clear Box, the app generates a random identifier on your device. This identifier is used to associate your settings and configurations locally. It is not your name, email, phone number, or Apple ID. We cannot use it to find out who you are.

**Your medication regimen.** The medicines you add to the app, the dose schedule, dose amounts, and any notes you write are stored locally on your device, encrypted using AES encryption. They are not stored on our server.

**Your dose logs.** When you mark a dose as taken or skipped, that record (date, time slot, medicine, status, optional notes) is stored locally on your device, encrypted using AES encryption. It is not stored on our server.

**Your reminder times.** The times of day you've set for morning, afternoon, evening, and night dose slots, plus an optional first name if you've entered one. These are stored locally on your device.

We do **not** collect: your name (unless you optionally enter a first name), email, phone number, location, contacts, photos, health data from Apple Health, advertising identifiers, or anything else not listed above.

## What we do with it

The medication regimen, dose logs, and reminder times are used solely to provide the app's functionality — showing you your medicines, reminding you when to take them, and tracking adherence on your device. We do not analyse this data. We do not sell it. We do not share it with advertisers, insurers, employers, or anyone else.

## Analytics (PostHog)

We use PostHog to collect limited, anonymous analytics to help us understand how the app is used and improve the user experience.
- **Opt-in only**: Analytics are disabled by default. We only collect this data if you explicitly opt-in.
- **Funnel events only**: We only track basic usage events, specifically: `consent_granted`, `profile_created`, `medicine_added`, and `dose_logged`.
- **Privacy-first configuration**: We strip all IP addresses and device metadata. No personal data (such as names or emails) or health data (such as specific medicine names, dosages, or notes) is ever sent to PostHog.
- **Hosting**: These analytics are processed and stored on PostHog Cloud's EU instance.

## Where your data is stored

Your data is stored locally and securely on your own device.
- On-device data is encrypted using AES encryption.
- No medication regimen, dose logs, or personal information is transmitted to or stored on our servers.

## How long we keep it

Your medication regimen and dose logs are stored on your device for as long as the app is installed. If you uninstall the app or use the "Delete my data" option in Settings, this data is deleted permanently and immediately from your device. We do not keep any copies.

## Your rights under UK GDPR

You have the right to:

- **Access and Export** the data we hold. Since your data is stored locally, you can export your data in a portable JSON format directly within the app settings.
- **Correct** inaccurate data. You can edit medicines, regimens, and logs directly in the app.
- **Delete** your data. You can delete all your data permanently and immediately at any time using the "Delete my data" option in Settings.
- **Object** to processing or **withdraw consent** at any time. You can withdraw your consent for analytics in Settings, and delete your local data if you wish to stop using the app.
- **Complain** to the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) at ico.org.uk or contact us directly at hello@clearbox.info if you are unhappy with how we have handled your data or if you have a data query.

Because your data is stored locally on your device, you can exercise these rights directly within the app settings without needing to contact us.

## Lawful basis

We process your medication data on the basis of your **explicit consent**, which you give on first launch and can withdraw at any time. Medication data is "special category" data under UK GDPR Article 9, and explicit consent is our lawful basis under Article 9(2)(a).

## Children

Clear Box is not directed at children under 13. If you are a parent or guardian and believe a child has used the app, contact us and we will ensure any associated data on the device is deleted.

## Security

Data is stored locally on your device and is encrypted at rest using AES encryption. No medication data is transmitted over the internet or stored on our servers.

We are a small operation. Because we do not store your health or medication data on our servers, the risk of a server-side breach of your sensitive data is eliminated.

## Third parties

The app itself contains no third-party tracking or advertising SDKs other than the opt-in PostHog analytics integration described above. Our search system uses Google Custom Search to find patient information leaflets — these searches contain medicine names, not your device identifier or personal data. Google sees the medicine name; the search query comes from our backend server's IP address, not yours.

## Medical disclaimer

Clear Box is **not a medical device**. It is an information and tracking tool. The information shown about medicines is sourced from the UK NHS dictionary of medicines and devices (dm+d) and from licensed patient information leaflets, but the app does not give medical advice. Always consult your GP, pharmacist, or NHS 111 for medical questions. Do not change your medication based on what the app shows you.

## Changes to this policy

If we change this policy, we will update the date at the top and, for any change that materially affects your rights, prompt you to review the new policy on your next app launch.
